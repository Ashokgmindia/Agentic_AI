## 1. Vision & Strategic Alignment

### 1.1. Vision Statement

To empower our organization with a centralized, intelligent, and user-friendly platform for managing all customer and prospect interactions, thereby fostering stronger relationships, driving sales growth, and improving operational efficiency.

### 1.2. Business Goals

*   **Enhance Customer Relationship Management:** Improve the quality and depth of customer interactions by providing a unified view of all communications and history.
*   **Increase Sales Productivity:** Streamline sales processes, automate repetitive tasks, and provide sales teams with better insights to close more deals faster.
*   **Improve Data Accuracy and Accessibility:** Ensure reliable, up-to-date contact information is readily available to all relevant departments.
*   **Streamline Marketing Efforts:** Facilitate targeted marketing campaigns through better segmentation and understanding of customer profiles.
*   **Boost Operational Efficiency:** Reduce manual data entry and administrative overhead associated with managing contact information and interactions.

### 1.3. Alignment with Organizational Strategy

This Contact Management System directly supports the organization's strategic pillars of:
*   **Customer Centricity:** By providing tools to better understand and engage with customers.
*   **Operational Excellence:** By streamlining internal processes and improving data management.
*   **Growth and Innovation:** By enabling sales and marketing teams to perform more effectively and identify new opportunities.

## 2. Project Scope

### 2.1. In-Scope Areas

*   Centralized database for contacts (customers, prospects, partners).
*   Tracking of interactions (calls, emails, meetings).
*   Activity logging and history for each contact.
*   Basic search and filtering capabilities.
*   User roles and permissions management.
*   Reporting on contact data and interaction activities.
*   Data import/export functionality (e.g., CSV).

### 2.2. Out-of-Scope Areas

*   Full CRM functionality (e.g., complex sales pipeline management, advanced forecasting).
*   Marketing automation (e.g., email campaign execution, lead scoring).
*   Customer support ticketing system.
*   Integration with all legacy systems (specific integrations to be defined).
*   Mobile application development (unless specified later).
*   Advanced analytics and AI-driven insights (beyond basic reporting).

### 2.3. Assumptions

*   Key stakeholders will be available for input and validation.
*   Existing IT infrastructure can support the new system.
*   Data quality in existing sources is reasonably manageable for migration.
*   Users will receive adequate training to adopt the new system.
*   Necessary budget and resources will be allocated.

### 2.4. Constraints

*   **Budget:** To be defined.
*   **Timeline:** To be defined.
*   **Technology Stack:** May be constrained by existing enterprise standards.
*   **Compliance:** Must adhere to relevant data privacy regulations (e.g., GDPR, CCPA, etc.).
*   **Integration:** Limited to specific, prioritized integrations.

## 4. High-Level Features / Capabilities

*   **Contact Management:** Create, read, update, delete (CRUD) operations for contacts and organizations.
*   **Interaction Tracking:** Log calls, emails, meetings, and other relevant touchpoints.
*   **Activity History:** Chronological view of all interactions associated with a contact.
*   **Search & Filtering:** Robust search by name, company, email, phone, tags, etc.
*   **Custom Fields:** Ability to add specific data fields relevant to the business.
*   **Reporting Dashboard:** Overview of key metrics (e.g., number of contacts, recent activities).
*   **User Management:** Administer user accounts, roles, and permissions.
*   **Data Import/Export:** Bulk import of contacts and export for analysis or backup.

## 5. Risks & Dependencies

### 5.1. Strategic Risks

*   **Low User Adoption:** Risk that users find the system difficult to use or not beneficial, leading to underutilization.
    *   *Mitigation:* Comprehensive training, change management, user involvement in design, intuitive UI/UX.
*   **Data Migration Issues:** Inaccurate or incomplete data transfer from legacy systems.
    *   *Mitigation:* Thorough data cleansing plan, phased migration, validation checks.
*   **Integration Challenges:** Difficulties in connecting with other critical business systems.
    *   *Mitigation:* Clearly define integration points early, use standard APIs where possible, dedicated integration testing.
*   **Scope Creep:** Uncontrolled expansion of project scope beyond initial objectives.
    *   *Mitigation:* Strict change control process, clear definition of MVP, phased rollout.

### 5.2. Dependencies

*   **Availability of Key Stakeholders:** Crucial for requirements gathering and validation.
*   **Existing CRM/ERP Systems:** For potential data migration or integration.
*   **IT Infrastructure:** Server capacity, network, security protocols.
*   **Data Governance Policies:** Adherence to organizational data standards.

## 6. Success Metrics (KPIs/OKRs)

*   **Increase in Lead Conversion Rate:** Measure the percentage of leads converted to customers.
*   **Reduction in Data Entry Errors:** Track the rate of inaccuracies in contact records.
*   **Improved Customer Satisfaction Scores (CSAT):** Measure customer feedback related to interactions.
*   **User Adoption Rate:** Percentage of target users actively using the system daily/weekly.
*   **Time Spent on Administrative Tasks:** Reduction in time spent by sales/support on manual data management.
*   **Data Completeness:** Percentage of contact records with essential fields populated.
